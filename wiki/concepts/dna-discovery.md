# DNA Discovery

**Definition:** The scientific discovery and interpretation of DNA's molecular structure as the basis for hereditary information transfer in living systems.

**Also known as:** Discovery of DNA structure, double-helix discovery, molecular-structure discovery of DNA.

## Overview
[coverage: medium]

DNA discovery matters in this repository because it shows how structural chemistry becomes biological explanation. The importance was not only that scientists identified a molecule called DNA, but that they understood enough about its molecular architecture to explain how biological information could be stored and transferred. In that sense, the history belongs naturally beside [[Carbon in Biology and DNA]] rather than as an isolated history-of-science anecdote.

For GraphWiki, this page serves a precise role. It does not try to become a full genetics-history branch. Instead, it marks the point where carbon-based molecular structure, information transfer, and life science become inseparable. The Nobel summary for the 1962 prize is a clean anchor because it emphasizes the significance of the molecular structure of nucleic acids for information transfer in living material.
