# Phase Transition

**Definition:** A transformation in which a system changes from one phase to another because conditions such as temperature, pressure, or composition shift the balance of stability.

**Also known as:** Phase change, phase transformation.

## Overview
[coverage: medium]

Phase transitions are where thermodynamic stability and kinetic pathway meet. A system may change from solid to liquid, liquid to gas, or from one solid-state arrangement to another when external conditions move it across a stability boundary. These changes are central to physics, chemistry, and materials science because they often produce dramatic changes in structure and properties.

In the simplest cases, phase transitions are represented through [[Phase Diagram]] boundaries that show where one phase ceases to be stable and another becomes favored. But the real process of transformation is often more complicated than a stability map suggests. The transition may depend on nucleation, diffusion, interfacial effects, and processing history, which is why [[Kinetics]] matters alongside [[Thermodynamics]].

For GraphWiki, phase transition is useful because it ties together abstract thermodynamic reasoning and applied materials behavior. It explains why phase diagrams matter, why heat treatment changes materials, and why equilibrium predictions do not automatically determine what structure is observed at a given moment.

## Key Properties

- Triggered by changing temperature, pressure, composition, or other control variables
- Can occur between familiar phases or between different solid-state structures
- Often represented in equilibrium form through phase diagrams
- Real transformation pathways depend on nucleation, growth, and transport kinetics
- Frequently linked to large changes in material properties

## Relationships

- **Is-a:** Thermodynamic and materials transformation process
- **Has-part:** Stability change, nucleation, growth, transformation pathway
- **Related:** [[Phase Diagram]], [[Thermodynamics]], [[Kinetics]], [[Materials Science]]
- **Used-by:** Alloy design, processing science, condensed matter, thermal treatment analysis

## Examples

- Melting and freezing in a pure substance under changing temperature conditions
- A solid-state transformation during alloy heat treatment
- A material moving across a phase boundary in a composition-temperature diagram
