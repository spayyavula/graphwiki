# Carbon in Energy

**Definition:** The role of carbon-containing fuels, materials, and reaction systems in energy generation, industrial conversion, emissions, and decarbonization.

**Also known as:** Carbon-energy systems, carbon in energy systems, energy-facing carbon science.

## Overview
[coverage: medium]

Carbon sits at the center of modern energy systems in more than one way. It appears as stored chemical energy in fossil fuels such as [[Coal]], as emitted carbon dioxide in combustion and process industries, and as an engineered material in catalytic and electrochemical energy technologies. That means Carbon Science needs an explicit energy-facing page rather than leaving energy references scattered across climate, catalysis, and materials pages.

This page is useful because it ties together several branches that might otherwise look unrelated. [[Coal]] represents legacy, geologic-carbon energy use. [[Carbon Capture and Storage]] represents an intervention layer designed to manage carbon emitted from those systems. [[Carbon Catalysis]] represents a more chemistry-driven role, where carbon materials help drive oxygen reduction, CO2 conversion, and other reaction pathways important to energy technologies. Together these show that carbon in energy is not just about fuels. It is about conversion, control, emissions, and engineered carbon function.

## Key Properties

- Includes carbon as fuel, emitted product, catalytic medium, and engineered energy material
- Connects combustion, industrial processing, electrocatalysis, and carbon-management strategy
- Makes the Carbon Science branch more application-facing and less limited to descriptive carbon-cycle framing
- Bridges climate, thermodynamics, industrial systems, and carbon-materials research
- Helps explain why carbon remains central even in decarbonization discussions

## Examples

- Coal combustion converts stored carbon-rich fuel into useful energy and carbon dioxide emissions
- Carbon capture systems are applied to large stationary emitters to separate and store concentrated CO2 streams
- Carbon-based catalysts are studied for oxygen reduction and CO2-conversion steps relevant to energy devices and environmental chemistry
