# Condensed Matter Physics

**Definition:** The branch of physics that studies the collective behavior of solids, liquids, soft matter, and other many-particle systems, especially how structure and interactions produce material properties.

**Also known as:** Condensed matter, solid-state physics in its narrower traditional form.

## Overview
[coverage: medium]

Condensed matter physics studies how large collections of atoms, electrons, spins, and other degrees of freedom produce observable material behavior. Instead of focusing mainly on chemical composition or fabrication workflow, it asks how structure, symmetry, interactions, excitations, and collective organization give rise to conductivity, magnetism, elasticity, superconductivity, phase transitions, and many other physical phenomena.

This is one of the main physics-facing entry points into the broader materials world. A materials-science course may emphasize processing, defects, phase diagrams, and engineering performance, while a condensed-matter course often emphasizes band structure, phonons, order parameters, broken symmetry, and emergent behavior. The overlap is still large, especially when the subject is taught by physics faculty or framed around solids and structure-property explanation.

For GraphWiki, [[Condensed Matter Physics]] helps make the user-requested perspective explicit. It clarifies that the Materials Science branch is not only about synthesis, chemistry, or applications. It also sits close to the physics of crystals, excitations, elasticity, transport, and transformations, which is why it links naturally to [[Materials Science]], [[Phase Transition]], [[Thermodynamics]], and [[Solid State Chemistry]].

## Key Properties

- Focuses on collective behavior in solids and other condensed phases of matter
- Explains properties through structure, interactions, symmetry, and excitations
- Strongly associated with crystals, defects, transport, magnetism, elasticity, and phase transitions
- Overlaps with materials science but is more physics-centered in framing and methods
- Often connects microscopic models to macroscopic observables and emergent behavior

## Relationships

- **Is-a:** Physics discipline, many-body and materials-focused field
- **Has-part:** Crystal physics, transport, lattice vibrations, magnetism, phase transitions, collective excitations
- **Related:** [[Materials Science]], [[Solid State Chemistry]], [[Phase Transition]], [[Thermodynamics]], [[Entropy]]
- **Used-by:** Physics-oriented teaching on materials, solid-state research, quantum materials, statistical and thermal analysis of matter

## Examples

- Explaining electrical conduction in a crystal through electronic structure rather than only chemical composition
- Studying phonons to understand heat capacity and thermal transport in a solid
- Using symmetry breaking and order parameters to describe a phase transition in a material system
