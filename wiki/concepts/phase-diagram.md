# Phase Diagram

**Definition:** A compact representation showing which phases of a material or system are stable under different conditions such as temperature, pressure, and composition.

**Also known as:** Equilibrium phase diagram, phase map.

## Overview
[coverage: medium]

Phase diagrams are one of the most useful representational tools in thermodynamics and materials science. They condense a large amount of stability information into a visual form, showing which phases are expected under different combinations of external conditions. Instead of listing many separate cases, a phase diagram lets a learner see regions of stability, coexistence boundaries, and critical transition points in one structure.

Their power comes from the fact that they translate thermodynamic reasoning into a form usable for real systems. In a one-component system, a phase diagram may show solid, liquid, and gas regions as temperature and pressure change. In a multicomponent material system, it may show how composition and temperature determine which phases appear, disappear, or coexist. This is why phase diagrams are central to alloy design, ceramic processing, and the thermodynamics of materials.

For GraphWiki, [[Phase Diagram]] is a particularly useful bridge concept. It links the abstract logic of [[Thermodynamics]] and [[Entropy]] with the applied reasoning of [[Materials Science]]. It also helps explain why temperature-dependent stability matters in materials processing and why the same substance or system can exhibit radically different behavior under different conditions.

## Key Properties

- Encodes phase stability across changing conditions such as temperature, pressure, and composition
- Shows boundaries between distinct stable regions and coexistence regimes
- Often used to interpret equilibrium and transformation behavior in material systems
- Connects thermodynamic state reasoning to practical material design and processing
- Can apply to simple one-component systems or more complex multicomponent materials

## Relationships

- **Is-a:** Thermodynamic and materials-science representation tool
- **Has-part:** Stability regions, coexistence lines, critical points, composition or pressure axes
- **Related:** [[Thermodynamics]], [[Entropy]], [[Materials Science]]
- **Used-by:** Alloy design, phase-stability analysis, materials processing, equilibrium teaching and visualization

## Examples

- A pressure-temperature diagram showing where water is stable as ice, liquid, or vapor
- A temperature-composition diagram used to understand alloy solidification behavior
- A materials-processing workflow that relies on phase boundaries to choose heat-treatment conditions
