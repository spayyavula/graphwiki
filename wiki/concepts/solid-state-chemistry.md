# Solid State Chemistry

**Definition:** The study of the composition, bonding, structure, and reactivity of solid materials, especially how atomic arrangement influences material behavior.

**Also known as:** Solid-state chemistry, chemistry of solids.

## Overview
[coverage: medium]

Solid-state chemistry focuses on how atoms and ions are arranged in solids and how those arrangements determine properties. It sits near the chemistry end of the materials spectrum, but it overlaps heavily with physics and materials science because solids are governed by both composition and structure. Crystals, defects, bonding patterns, and compositional substitution all play major roles in the behavior of solid materials.

The field is especially useful for GraphWiki because it provides a chemistry-centered bridge into [[Materials Science]]. Materials science tends to ask broader structure-property and processing questions across many material classes. Solid-state chemistry narrows the focus to the chemical logic of solids: how lattices form, how defects influence behavior, and how composition and bonding shape stability and functionality.

It also connects directly to [[Phase Diagram]] and [[Phase Transition]] reasoning. Many practical questions about synthesis, phase purity, and structure control in solids depend on thermodynamic stability and kinetic accessibility. That makes solid-state chemistry a natural cross-link between chemistry-oriented learning and the broader materials branch.

## Key Properties

- Focuses on bonding, composition, crystal structure, and defects in solids
- Strongly connected to inorganic chemistry, crystallography, and materials science
- Helps explain why atomic arrangement influences conductivity, stability, magnetism, and other properties
- Supports reasoning about synthesis, phase purity, and compositional substitution
- Connects chemistry-centered explanation to broader structure-property analysis

## Relationships

- **Is-a:** Chemistry-oriented study of materials and solids
- **Has-part:** Crystal structure, bonding, defects, composition, lattice behavior
- **Related:** [[Materials Science]], [[Phase Diagram]], [[Phase Transition]], [[Thermodynamics]]
- **Used-by:** Inorganic materials research, semiconductor chemistry, ceramic science, synthesis and characterization workflows

## Examples

- Explaining how crystal structure influences conductivity in a solid material
- Studying how compositional substitution changes phase stability and properties
- Using synthesis conditions to target a desired solid phase and avoid impurities
