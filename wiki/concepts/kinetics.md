# Kinetics

**Definition:** The study of how fast physical and chemical processes occur and what mechanisms or rate controls determine that speed.

**Also known as:** Reaction kinetics, chemical kinetics, rate theory.

## Overview
[coverage: medium]

Kinetics answers a different question from [[Thermodynamics]]. Thermodynamics asks whether a process is favored or what equilibrium state is expected under given conditions. Kinetics asks how quickly that process proceeds and what pathway it follows. A process can be thermodynamically favorable yet kinetically slow, which is why stable-looking systems can persist far from their lowest-energy state.

This distinction makes kinetics essential in chemistry, materials processing, combustion, and many real-world engineering systems. Reactions, crystallization, diffusion, nucleation, and phase changes all depend not only on energetic favorability but also on activation barriers, transport limits, and mechanistic pathways. For GraphWiki, kinetics is therefore a necessary bridge concept between [[Thermodynamics]] and [[Phase Transition]] reasoning.

In materials contexts, kinetics helps explain why processing history matters. Two systems with the same equilibrium prediction can end up with very different microstructures if cooling rates, diffusion times, or nucleation behavior differ. This is one reason kinetics is central to practical materials science even when thermodynamics defines the stability landscape.

## Key Properties

- Focuses on rate, mechanism, and pathway rather than only equilibrium state
- Often depends on activation barriers, temperature, diffusion, and transport constraints
- Explains why thermodynamically favored processes may still proceed slowly
- Important for reactions, phase changes, materials processing, and combustion
- Commonly paired with thermodynamics for complete process understanding

## Relationships

- **Is-a:** Core physical and chemical process framework
- **Has-part:** Rate laws, activation barriers, mechanistic steps, transport limits
- **Related:** [[Thermodynamics]], [[Phase Transition]], [[Materials Science]]
- **Used-by:** Reaction engineering, combustion analysis, materials processing, chemical and physical transformation studies

## Examples

- A reaction that is thermodynamically favored but proceeds negligibly at room temperature because of a high activation barrier
- A heat-treatment process where cooling rate changes the final material microstructure
- A phase transformation limited by diffusion rather than by equilibrium possibility
