# MIT OpenCourseWare

**Type:** Educational platform / open course repository

**Definition:** MIT OpenCourseWare is an open educational platform that is especially relevant to GraphWiki because it anchors major source material for both the Thermodynamics and Materials Science branches.

## Overview

MIT OpenCourseWare functions as one of the strongest source institutions in the current science-side GraphWiki branches. It appears repeatedly in the Thermodynamics and Materials Science source packs, providing accessible, university-backed entry points into equilibrium thermodynamics, kinetics, thermodynamics of materials, structure of materials, and introductory solid-state chemistry.

Its value in GraphWiki is not just that it hosts course pages. It also helps define branch structure. In the thermodynamics sources, MIT OpenCourseWare supports both introductory and advanced paths, including the bridge between classical thermodynamics and materials-oriented reasoning. In the materials sources, it provides the most consistent entry path across structure, bonding, and energetics. That makes it a natural entity-level anchor for learners moving from guide pages into deeper concept exploration.

Because the platform spans multiple courses instead of a single narrow topic, it also helps connect branches that would otherwise look artificially separate. The same institutional source base links [[Thermodynamics]], [[Kinetics]], [[Materials Science]], [[Solid State Chemistry]], and [[Phase Diagram]] reasoning into a coherent teaching-oriented path.

## Key Components

- **Thermodynamics sources:** Introductory and advanced thermodynamics course pages and materials
- **Materials sources:** Fundamentals of materials science, structure of materials, and thermodynamics of materials
- **Solid-state chemistry bridge:** Supports the chemistry-facing side of the materials branch
- **Learning-path role:** Provides beginner-friendly yet source-traceable entry points for multiple GraphWiki branches
