# ISO 20022

**Type:** Standard / Ontology

**Definition:** The international standard for financial industry messaging, providing a common XML-based platform with a formal [[OWL]] DL semantic layer (ISO/TR 22126-2:2025) spanning payments, securities, FX, trade finance, and cards.

## Overview

ISO 20022 is the global lingua franca for financial messaging, replacing legacy formats such as ISO 15022 and SWIFT MT with a rich, structured data model that carries formal semantic definitions. Unlike earlier tag-based message standards, ISO 20022 messages are built from a shared Business Dictionary and expressed in XML Schema (XSD), with a normative [[OWL]] DL ontology (ISO/TR 22126-2:2025) derived from the UML model via a formal mapping process. The full SWIFT cross-border payments migration to ISO 20022 MX format completed in November 2025, covering all major correspondent banking flows including cover payments, credit transfers, and financial institution transfers.

The standard spans five financial domains — Payments, Securities, Foreign Exchange, Trade Finance, and Cards — organized into message families identified by four-character business domain codes. Messages are versioned using codes like `pacs.008.001.10` (FI Credit Transfer, version 10). The `pain` family handles payment initiation, `pacs` covers interbank clearing and settlement, `camt` manages cash and account information, `sese` and `seev` handle securities settlement and events, and `reda` carries reference data. An [[RDF]]-based API at `iso20022.plus/api/rdf-2/` enables integration with [[Linked Data]] pipelines, and a community [[OWL]] representation is also available for ontology-based tooling.

ISO 20022 intersects with the broader financial [[Ontology]] ecosystem at multiple points. [[FIBO]] legal entity and instrument classes align with ISO 20022 message semantics, enabling mapping for regulatory reporting. [[GLEIF]] LEI codes appear natively in `pacs`, `camt`, and `sese` messages for identifying parties. [[XBRL]] reference data from `reda` messages can populate XBRL entity and instrument metadata. [[FpML]] and ISO 20022 overlap in trade reporting — EMIR Refit regulatory mandates often require both. [[FIX Protocol]] remains dominant for execution while ISO 20022 governs settlement and regulatory messaging, with translation mappings available. [[SPARQL]] queries over the RDF API enable cross-standard semantic analysis.

## Key Components

- **pain (Payment Initiation):** Customer-to-bank payment orders
- **pacs (Payments Clearing and Settlement):** Interbank credit transfers, including gpi overlay
- **camt (Cash Management):** Account statements, notifications, balance queries
- **sese / seev / semt:** Securities settlement instructions, corporate actions, safekeeping
- **fxtr:** FX trade confirmations and settlements
- **reda (Reference Data):** Party, security, and account master data
- **BAH (Business Application Header):** Common envelope for all MX messages
- **Data Dictionary elements:** `BranchAndFinancialInstitutionIdentification`, `CashAccount`, `FinancialInstrumentIdentification`, `PartyIdentification`, `RemittanceInformation`

- **Built on:** [[OWL]], [[RDF]], [[W3C]] standards; UML source model

- **Used by:** SWIFT TARGET2, T2S, central bank RTGS systems globally
