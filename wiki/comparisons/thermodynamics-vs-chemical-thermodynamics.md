# Thermodynamics vs Chemical Thermodynamics

[[Thermodynamics]] is the broader framework for energy, entropy, equilibrium, and process constraints across physical systems. [[Chemical Thermodynamics]] is the chemistry-facing part of that framework, focused on reactions, mixtures, free energy, equilibrium constants, and chemical potential.

## Comparison Table

| Dimension | [[Thermodynamics]] | [[Chemical Thermodynamics]] |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary scope** | General framework for heat, work, energy, entropy, and equilibrium | Chemistry-centered treatment of reactions, mixtures, free energy, and equilibrium composition |
| **Typical questions** | Is a process possible or favored under given conditions? | What reaction direction is favored and what composition is at equilibrium? |
| **Common variables** | Energy, entropy, temperature, pressure, volume | Gibbs free energy, chemical potential, activities, equilibrium constants, partial molar quantities |
| **Typical settings** | Physics, engineering, materials, energy systems | Physical chemistry, combustion, solution chemistry, electrochemistry, materials chemistry |
| **View of equilibrium** | Broad state and stability perspective | Equilibrium of species, phases, and reactions in multicomponent systems |
| **Relation** | Umbrella field | Specialized branch inside thermodynamics |

## Thermodynamics as the Umbrella

[[Thermodynamics]] applies across many kinds of systems. It includes the logic of heat engines, phase stability, entropy production, materials behavior, and system constraints. Its strength is generality: it provides the large-scale rules that govern what changes are possible and what states are stable.

## Chemical Thermodynamics as the Chemistry Layer

[[Chemical Thermodynamics]] narrows the focus to substances and reactions. It emphasizes Gibbs free energy, reaction spontaneity, equilibrium constants, chemical potential, and mixture behavior. This is the layer that usually makes thermodynamics feel concrete in chemistry courses, because it connects the abstract framework to real reacting systems.

## Why the Distinction Helps

For GraphWiki, separating the two clarifies that the Thermodynamics branch should not read only like a general physics summary. It also needs the chemistry-facing equilibrium story that many learners expect: reaction direction, multicomponent systems, phase coexistence, and free-energy reasoning.
