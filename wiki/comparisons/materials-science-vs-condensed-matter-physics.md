# Materials Science vs Condensed Matter Physics

[[Materials Science]] and [[Condensed Matter Physics]] overlap heavily, especially when the topic is taught through structure-property reasoning. The difference is mainly in framing. Materials science is the broader field connecting composition, processing, structure, defects, mechanics, and performance. Condensed matter physics is the more physics-centered study of collective behavior, excitations, symmetry, transport, and phase transitions in material systems.

## Comparison Table

| Dimension | [[Materials Science]] | [[Condensed Matter Physics]] |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary scope** | Broad field covering structure, processing, defects, properties, and performance | Physics-centered study of collective behavior and material properties in condensed phases |
| **Typical questions** | How do composition, processing, and structure determine usable material behavior? | How do interactions, symmetry, and excitations produce observed physical behavior? |
| **Disciplinary center** | Interdisciplinary: chemistry, physics, mechanics, and engineering | Primarily physics, especially statistical, solid-state, and many-body reasoning |
| **Common tools** | Phase diagrams, thermodynamics, mechanics, characterization, microstructure analysis | Hamiltonians, band structure, phonons, order parameters, transport, scattering, statistical mechanics |
| **Relation to applications** | Often tied directly to fabrication, design, and performance goals | Often aims first at physical explanation, though many results feed materials design |
| **Overlap zone** | Defects, elasticity, phase behavior, transport, crystal structure | Defects, elasticity, phase behavior, transport, crystal structure |

## Materials Science as the Broader Umbrella

[[Materials Science]] includes the chemistry of what a material is made of, the engineering of how it is processed, and the physics of how its internal structure drives behavior. That makes it broader in practical scope. It routinely connects microscopic explanation to manufacturability, reliability, and performance under real constraints.

## Condensed Matter Physics as the Physics Angle

[[Condensed Matter Physics]] usually centers on explanation rather than processing workflow. It asks how electronic states, lattice vibrations, symmetry breaking, spin behavior, and collective organization produce phenomena such as conductivity, magnetism, superconductivity, or structural transformation. That is why a materials topic taught by physics professors can feel like the same terrain viewed from a different disciplinary angle.

## Why the Distinction Helps

For GraphWiki, this distinction prevents the Materials Science branch from collapsing into either a purely chemistry-and-processing topic or a purely theoretical physics topic. It shows that the branch needs both perspectives: the engineering breadth of [[Materials Science]] and the structure-and-collective-behavior lens of [[Condensed Matter Physics]].
