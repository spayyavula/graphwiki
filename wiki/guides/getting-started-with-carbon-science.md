# Getting Started with Carbon Science

This guide provides a first path through the Carbon Science branch of GraphWiki. The current topic is intentionally broad, so the learning path starts with the carbon cycle and then branches into carbon management and carbon-based materials.

## Prerequisites

You do not need deep prior knowledge, but it helps to be comfortable with the idea of connected systems and scientific modeling. The topic also connects naturally to [[Materials Science Academic Source Guide]] and [[Thermodynamics Academic Source Guide]].

---

## Step 1: Start with the System

Begin with [[Carbon Cycle]]. This is the organizing concept for the topic because it explains where carbon is stored, how it moves, and why changes in those flows matter for climate and earth systems.

---

## Step 2: Move to Intervention and Management

Read [[Carbon Capture and Storage]] next. This shows how carbon science moves from description to intervention, and why engineering solutions have to be evaluated within larger carbon-system constraints.

---

## Step 3: Branch into Carbon Materials

Read [[Carbon Allotrope]] to see the materials side of the field. This is the point where Carbon Science stops being only about climate and begins to overlap with nanoscience, electronic materials, and structure-property reasoning.

---

## Step 4: Add the Energy and Biology Branches

Read [[Coal]], [[Carbon in Energy]], [[Carbon in Biology and DNA]], and [[DNA Discovery]] next. These pages broaden the topic in two useful directions. Coal and the energy page make the industrial and decarbonization side of carbon explicit, while the biology and DNA pages show that carbon science also reaches into the molecular basis of life and the discovery history that made DNA scientifically central.

---

## Step 5: Go Deeper into Carbon Materials

Continue with [[Fullerenes]], [[Carbon-Carbon Composites]], [[Carbon Catalysis]], [[Activated Carbon]], and [[Graphene in Semiconductors]]. These pages make the materials branch more realistic. Fullerenes complete the allotrope side of the topic, carbon-carbon composites connect carbon materials directly to aerospace engineering and aircraft-brake applications, carbon catalysis shows that carbon can also be chemically active rather than only structural, activated carbon makes the surface-and-support branch explicit, and the graphene page makes the semiconductor-facing branch explicit.

---

## Step 6: Use the Source Pack to Choose a Deeper Direction

After those core pages, use [[Carbon Science Academic Source Guide]] to decide whether to go deeper into carbon-cycle science, carbon management, or carbon-based materials. That is the current branching point for the topic in this repository.
