# Getting Started with Materials Science

This guide provides a first path through the Materials Science branch of GraphWiki. The aim is to connect structure, chemistry, physics, phase behavior, and thermodynamics before branching into more specialized materials topics.

## Prerequisites

You do not need prior materials training, but it helps to be comfortable with the idea that atomic structure and processing history influence large-scale behavior. This branch overlaps strongly with [[Thermodynamics]] and [[Solid State Chemistry]], and it also has a clear physics-facing side through structure, defects, vibrations, and phase transitions.

---

## Step 1: Start with the Big Picture

Begin with [[Materials Science]]. This frames the field as the study of how composition, structure, processing, and energetics determine properties and performance.

---

## Step 2: Learn the Chemistry View of Solids

Read [[Solid State Chemistry]] next. This provides the chemistry-centered perspective on crystal structure, bonding, and composition that sits beneath much of materials reasoning.

---

## Step 3: Add the Physics and Structure View

Return to [[Materials Science]] with the physics angle in mind, then read [[Condensed Matter Physics]]. Focus on how crystal structure, defects, elasticity, vibrations, thermal response, and collective behavior affect material properties. This is the part of the field that often overlaps with how physicists teach solids and phase behavior.

---

## Step 4: Move into Stability and Transformation

Study [[Phase Diagram]] and [[Phase Transition]]. These pages explain how materials move between stable arrangements and why temperature, composition, and processing conditions matter so much.

---

## Step 5: Bring in Thermodynamics and Kinetics

Return to [[Thermodynamics]], [[Entropy]], and [[Kinetics]] to understand why a material may be stable, metastable, or slow to transform. This is where the branch becomes much more than a catalog of material types.

---

## Step 6: Use the Source Guide to Go Deeper

After the core pages, use [[Materials Science Academic Source Guide]] to branch toward structure of materials, thermodynamics of materials, phase behavior, solid-state chemistry, or the mechanics-facing side of the field.
