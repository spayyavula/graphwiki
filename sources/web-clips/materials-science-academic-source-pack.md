# Materials Science Academic Source Pack

This source pack collects verified academic and university-hosted material for building out the Materials Science topic in GraphWiki. The emphasis is on structure, phase behavior, and thermodynamics of materials.

## MIT

- **Fundamentals of Materials Science**
  - Institution: MIT OpenCourseWare
  - URL: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/3-012-fundamentals-of-materials-science-fall-2005/
  - Type: Course page
  - Why it is useful: A strong general introduction to the structure, energetics, and bonding foundations of materials science.

- **Structure of Materials**
  - Institution: MIT OpenCourseWare
  - URL: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/3-012sx-structure-of-materials-spring-2019/
  - Type: Course page
  - Why it is useful: Useful for the structural side of materials science and how different material classes are represented and compared.

- **Introduction to Solid-State Chemistry — Lecture 1**
  - Institution: MIT OpenCourseWare
  - URL: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/3-091-introduction-to-solid-state-chemistry-fall-2018/resources/lecture-1/
  - Type: Lecture material
  - Why it is useful: A readable entry point for composition, bonding, and material structure.

- **Thermodynamics of Materials**
  - Institution: MIT OpenCourseWare
  - URL: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/3-020-thermodynamics-of-materials-spring-2021/
  - Type: Course page
  - Why it is useful: Provides direct materials-thermodynamics coverage for phase behavior and energetic modeling.

## Berkeley

- **MATSCI 101 Course**
  - Institution: UC Berkeley Graduate Catalog
  - URL: https://graduate.catalog.berkeley.edu/courses/1147821
  - Type: Course catalog page
  - Why it is useful: Useful as a verified Berkeley anchor for a materials-science topic pathway.

- **Kristin A. Persson E40 Fall 2020 Thermodynamics Instructor**
  - Institution: UC Berkeley (course syllabus mirror)
  - URL: https://tbp.studentorg.berkeley.edu/syllabi/1480/download/
  - Type: Course syllabus
  - Why it is useful: Explicitly points to thermodynamics-in-materials texts and gives a materials-focused thermodynamics reading path.

- **Introduction to Continuum Mechanics**
  - Institution: UC Berkeley Computational Solid Mechanics Lab
  - URL: https://csml.berkeley.edu/Notes/ME185.pdf
  - Type: Lecture notes
  - Why it is useful: Useful for the mechanics branch of materials science, especially where continuum descriptions connect to material behavior.

## Caltech

- **Vibrational Thermodynamics of Materials**
  - Institution: Caltech
  - URL: https://www.its.caltech.edu/~matsci/btfgrp/Review_Main.pdf
  - Type: Review article
  - Why it is useful: One of the strongest sources in this pack, covering thermodynamic behavior of materials at substantial depth.

- **Essentials of T-c Phase Diagrams**
  - Institution: Caltech
  - URL: https://www.its.caltech.edu/~matsci/btf/PTM_Book/chapter2.pdf
  - Type: Book chapter
  - Why it is useful: Gives a compact and practical entry point into phase diagrams, one of the core representational tools in materials science.

- **Phase Transitions in Materials: Advanced Topics**
  - Institution: CaltechAUTHORS
  - URL: https://authors.library.caltech.edu/records/zxv32-pad14
  - Type: Book landing page / research monograph record
  - Why it is useful: Useful for advanced coverage of the thermodynamics and kinetics of phase transitions.

- **Molecular Thermodynamics**
  - Institution: Caltech Library listing PDF
  - URL: https://calteches.library.caltech.edu/2813/1/books.pdf
  - Type: Archival reference
  - Why it is useful: Connects molecular thermodynamics to phase changes and chemical systems, useful for cross-linking materials and thermodynamics.

## Notes

- The strongest introductory sources are MIT Fundamentals of Materials Science and MIT Structure of Materials.
- The strongest advanced sources are Caltech's Vibrational Thermodynamics of Materials and Phase Transitions in Materials.
- This pack is enough to support a starter materials-science branch, but it will benefit from a second pass focused on Berkeley, MIT, and Princeton materials labs.
