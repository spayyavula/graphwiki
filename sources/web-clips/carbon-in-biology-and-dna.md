# Carbon in Biology and DNA

- **Primary source:** National Human Genome Research Institute, *Deoxyribonucleic Acid (DNA)*
- **URL:** https://www.genome.gov/genetics-glossary/Deoxyribonucleic-Acid-DNA

## Key Takeaways

- DNA is a carbon-containing biomolecule whose information-carrying structure depends on a sugar-phosphate backbone and carbon-containing bases.
- The deoxyribose sugar in DNA is a direct reminder that carbon chemistry is foundational to biological information storage, not only to materials and fuels.
- Carbon Science can therefore branch toward biology as well as toward climate, catalysis, and materials.
- A biology-facing carbon page should stay scoped to carbon's chemical role in biomolecular structure rather than trying to become a full genomics branch.

## Why It Matters For GraphWiki

- Justifies a dedicated [[Carbon in Biology and DNA]] concept in the Carbon Science topic.
- Broadens the branch so carbon is not represented only as atmospheric carbon, engineered carbon, or structural carbon.
- Gives the wiki a clean way to acknowledge the user's DNA-related request without expanding prematurely into a full biology topic tree.
