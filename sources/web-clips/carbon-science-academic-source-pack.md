# Carbon Science Academic Source Pack

This source pack collects verified academic and university-hosted material for building out the Carbon Science topic in GraphWiki. The emphasis is on the carbon cycle, climate-linked carbon science, carbon capture, and carbon-based materials.

## Carbon cycle and climate foundations

- **Background: Forest Carbon and Forest Carbon Management**
  - Institution: Michigan State University
  - URL: https://www.canr.msu.edu/fccp/FCCP-ORL/Background_Forest%20Carbon%20Management.pdf
  - Type: Background guide / teaching PDF
  - Why it is useful: Gives a practical introduction to carbon sources, sinks, and forest-carbon management.

- **A US Carbon Cycle Science Plan**
  - Institution: UCAR / CPAESS
  - URL: https://cpaess.ucar.edu/sites/default/files/2025-06/us-carbon-cycle-science-plan-2011.pdf
  - Type: Science plan
  - Why it is useful: A strong planning-level source for major carbon pools, fluxes, greenhouse gases, and measurement priorities.

- **The Global Carbon Cycle: A Test of Our Knowledge**
  - Institution: Rutgers University
  - URL: https://ebme.marine.rutgers.edu/HistoryEarthSystems/HistEarthSystems_Fall2008/Week11b/Falkowski_et_al_Science_2000.pdf
  - Type: Academic paper
  - Why it is useful: A foundational synthesis of the global carbon cycle and the limits of current understanding.

- **Lecture 27: The Carbon Cycle and Earth's Climate**
  - Institution: University of Hawaii System
  - URL: https://www.soest.hawaii.edu/krubin/GG325/lect27.pdf
  - Type: Lecture notes
  - Why it is useful: Useful for beginner-facing explanation of anthropogenic carbon-cycle disruption and climate effects.

- **The Global Carbon Cycle**
  - Institution: University of Washington
  - URL: https://courses.washington.edu/pcc588/lectures_notes/588_09_Ccycle_js40_Pt1.pdf
  - Type: Lecture notes
  - Why it is useful: Adds a geoscience-oriented explanation of long-term carbon storage and fluxes.

## Carbon capture and management

- **Carbon Capture and Storage: Discovering the Science of CO2 Capture and Storage**
  - Institution: University of California, Berkeley
  - URL: https://vcresearch.berkeley.edu/sites/default/files/wysiwyg/filemanager/Philomathia_symposium_presentations/B_Smit.pdf
  - Type: Presentation PDF
  - Why it is useful: A good entry point into the scientific logic of carbon capture and storage.

## Carbon materials and allotropes

- **Carbon Nanotubes and Related Nanomaterials**
  - Institution: DSpace@MIT
  - URL: https://dspace.mit.edu/bitstream/handle/1721.1/134917/acsnano.8b06511.pdf?sequence=2&isAllowed=y
  - Type: Review article
  - Why it is useful: Provides a strong materials-side view of carbon science through nanotubes and related nanomaterials.

- **Allotropes of Carbon**
  - Institution: Purdue University
  - URL: https://www.physics.purdue.edu/psas/docs/Allotropes%20of%20Carbon.pdf
  - Type: Teaching PDF
  - Why it is useful: Useful for explaining the structure/property differences between graphite, diamond, graphene, and fullerene-like forms.

## Notes

- This topic name is broad, so this first pack deliberately covers three branches: carbon cycle and climate, carbon capture, and carbon materials.
- A later pass should deepen the topic either toward climate-carbon systems or toward carbon materials and nanoscience, depending on how GraphWiki wants to frame the topic selector.
