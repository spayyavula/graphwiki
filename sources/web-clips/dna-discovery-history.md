# DNA Discovery History

- **Primary source 1:** Nobel Prize, *The Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine 1962*
- **URL:** https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/1962/summary/
- **Primary source 2:** NHGRI, *Deoxyribonucleic Acid (DNA)*
- **URL:** https://www.genome.gov/genetics-glossary/Deoxyribonucleic-Acid-DNA

## Key Takeaways

- The 1962 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine recognized Francis Crick, James Watson, and Maurice Wilkins for discoveries concerning the molecular structure of nucleic acids and its significance for information transfer in living material.
- A DNA-discovery page in GraphWiki should focus on why the double-helix model mattered scientifically: it connected molecular structure to biological information.
- This history belongs beside [[Carbon in Biology and DNA]] because DNA discovery is one of the clearest demonstrations that carbon-based molecular structure can encode life-relevant information.

## Why It Matters For GraphWiki

- Supports a dedicated [[DNA Discovery]] concept page rather than leaving the historical thread implicit.
- Gives the Carbon Science branch a more complete biology-facing extension by pairing molecular structure with discovery history.
