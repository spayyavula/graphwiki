## Key Takeaways

- Coal is a carbon-rich sedimentary fuel formed from ancient plant matter over geologic time, which makes it a clear example of long-stored carbon entering modern energy systems.
- Coal rank matters because anthracite, bituminous, subbituminous, and lignite differ in carbon content and heating value.
- Coal remains relevant to electricity generation, industrial heat, and metallurgical coke production even as its relative share of some energy systems declines.
- Coal sits naturally in Carbon Science because it links geologic carbon storage, combustion, CO2 emissions, and carbon-management strategies such as [[Carbon Capture and Storage]].
- Energy use of coal cannot be separated from environmental context: burning coal produces CO2 along with sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, particulates, and other pollutants.
