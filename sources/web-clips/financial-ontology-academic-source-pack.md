# Financial Ontology Academic Source Pack

This source pack collects verified academic and university-hosted material for building out the Financial Ontology topic in GraphWiki. The emphasis is on financial knowledge graphs, semantic representation of financial data, and university-hosted materials that connect finance, AI, and structured knowledge.

## Columbia

- **Final Poster**
  - Institution: Columbia University Data Science Institute
  - URL: https://datascience.columbia.edu/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/05_Accenture-Banka_KnowledgeGraph_Poster.pdf
  - Type: Poster / applied research artifact
  - Why it is useful: Shows a real cryptocurrency-domain knowledge graph question-answering system, useful as an applied financial KG example.

- **Where Large Language Models Fall Short on Financial Knowledge**
  - Institution: Columbia Center for Financial Engineering / Bloomberg-Columbia material
  - URL: https://cfe.columbia.edu/sites/cfe.columbia.edu/files/content/slides/2025/%5BBloomberg-Columbia%5D%20Chava%20Financial%20Knowledge%20Gap%20in%20LLMs.pdf
  - Type: Slide deck / research presentation
  - Why it is useful: Connects LLM limitations, financial documents, and knowledge-graph-based evaluation, directly relevant to GraphWiki's LLM-wiki direction.

- **E6893 Big Data Analytics Lecture 4**
  - Institution: Columbia University Electrical Engineering
  - URL: https://www.ee.columbia.edu/~cylin/course/bigdata/EECS6893-BigDataAnalytics-Lecture4.pdf
  - Type: Lecture PDF
  - Why it is useful: Explicitly references constructing financial knowledge graphs as part of market intelligence workflows.

- **E6893 Big Data Analytics Lecture 11**
  - Institution: Columbia University Electrical Engineering
  - URL: http://www.ee.columbia.edu/~cylin/course/bigdata/EECS6893-BigDataAnalytics-Lecture11.pdf
  - Type: Lecture PDF
  - Why it is useful: Discusses storing financial data, regulations, reports, and metadata in a unified knowledge graph and defining semantics over the domain.

- **Project Report: Optimizing Financial Contagion Modeling**
  - Institution: Columbia University Computer Science Department
  - URL: https://www.cs.columbia.edu/~sedwards/classes/2024/4995-fall/reports/ShockNet-report.pdf
  - Type: Project report
  - Why it is useful: Useful for systemic-risk and network-based finance modeling, which broadens the financial ontology topic beyond identifiers and standards.
