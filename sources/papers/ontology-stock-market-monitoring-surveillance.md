# Ontology-Driven Framework for Stock Market Monitoring and Surveillance

**Published in:** Handbook of Global Financial Markets (World Scientific, 2018)
**URL:** https://www.worldscientific.com/doi/abs/10.1142/9789813236653_0004
**ResearchGate:** https://www.researchgate.net/publication/256063923

## Summary

Presents a domain ontology for stock market surveillance derived from SEC enforcement cases and market regulation documents. The ontology models market participants, financial instruments, market events, and suspicious trading patterns to enable automated surveillance.

## Ontology Scope

- **Market participants:** Brokers, traders, issuers, investors, regulators
- **Instruments:** Equities, options, bonds, derivatives
- **Events:** Trades, orders, cancellations, price movements, corporate announcements
- **Suspicious patterns:** Wash trading, spoofing, insider trading, pump-and-dump

## Construction Method

- Domain ontology derived from SEC fraud enforcement cases
- Market regulation documents used to define normal vs. anomalous behavior
- Pattern detection encoded as ontology axioms and rules

## Key Contributions

- One of the earliest formal ontologies specifically for market surveillance
- SEC case law as ontology source provides regulatory grounding
- Suspicious pattern modeling bridges ontology and compliance use cases

## Relevance to GraphWiki

- Market participant and instrument taxonomy directly usable
- Event modeling approach applicable to trade and order graph edges
- Regulatory/compliance angle relevant to surveillance-oriented query patterns
- Historical reference establishing the ontology-for-markets research thread
