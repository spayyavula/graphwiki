# Carbon-carbon composites and aircraft brakes source note

- **Primary overview article:** Agarwal et al., *An overview of carbon-carbon composite materials and their applications* (Frontiers in Materials, 2024)
- **DOI:** 10.3389/fmats.2024.1374034
- **Why it is useful:** Provides a recent overview of C/C composite properties, processing, coatings, tribology, aerospace applications, and application examples such as airplane braking systems, rocket nozzles, and heat shields.
- **Key extracted points:**
  - Carbon-carbon composites combine carbon fibers with a carbon matrix and are valued for low density, high-temperature strength retention, thermal shock resistance, and strong friction/wear behavior.
  - Aircraft braking systems are a canonical aerospace application because C/C composites absorb heat effectively, remain dimensionally stable at high temperature, and provide strong frictional performance.
  - Oxidation remains a central materials challenge, which is why coatings, densification routes, and microstructure control matter.
- **Aircraft-brake-specific references surfaced inside the 2024 overview:**
  - Awasthi and Wood, *Carbon/carbon composite materials for aircraft brakes*.
  - Buchgraber, *Carbon/carbon composite friction discs for aerospace*.
- **Supplementary review cue from search results:** Southern Illinois University review material reports that a large share of produced carbon-carbon composites are used in aircraft brakes, reinforcing the importance of this application area in the branch.
